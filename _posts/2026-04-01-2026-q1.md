## Finally Free

The biggest news of this quarter is simple: I’m finally done with military service. After spending so much time on top of the mountain, it still feels a little unreal to say it so casually. Now that it’s actually over, I mostly just feel relieved.

Looking back, service was exhausting in a lot of obvious ways, but it also gave me a very clear sense of what I don’t want my life to feel like. Being free again makes ordinary things feel valuable in a new way.

---

## Back To SNU

I came back to SNU this quarter as a sophomore. Returning to school after two years feels strange in its own way. Part of me still feels disconnected from campus life, like I’m stepping back into something that kept moving while I was gone. At the same time, I’m glad to be here again. It feels like I’ve re-entered my real life.

I’m not fully settled yet, but I do feel a quiet sense of momentum. Just being back in an academic environment, seeing other students, and having the chance to build again from here already means a lot.

---

## Alone, But Not Lonely

These days, I feel pretty lone, but not in a painful way. I spend a lot of time by myself but I don’t feel lonely. Mostly trying to keep a good relationship with myself.

There’s still a lot of uncertainty about what comes next, and I wouldn’t say everything feels bright or fully figured out. More than anything, this quarter felt like I had teleported. Everything around me had changed except myself.
